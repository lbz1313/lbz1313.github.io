What led to the modern electric tattoo machine?

The modern electric tattoo machine was developed from Thomas Edison's 1876 invention known as the “electric pen.” The device was originally designed to duplicate documents by rapidly puncturing paper.

In 1891, New York tattoo artist Samuel O'Reilly adapted Edison's electric pen into a tattooing device. This innovation made tattooing faster and more efficient than traditional hand methods such as stick-and-poke, and it became the foundation for the electric tattoo machines used today.
