Mission Statement

The Sacred Ink Museum is dedicated to the collection, preservation, and interpretation of the history of tattoos and showcases tattoos as a distinct form of graphic art and visual communication. Through original flash sheets, tools, and archival materials, the museum examines how tattoo imagery is created, circulated, and adapted across time.

Vision Statement

The Sacred Ink Museum envisions a future in which tattooing is widely recognized as a vital form of graphic art and visual communication—one that carries personal meaning, collective memory, and cultural knowledge. The museum seeks to elevate tattoo history within museum spaces by honoring the artists, communities, and visual languages that have shaped tattooing across time and place.

Core Values

Artistry & Visual Language, Cultural Context & History, Preservation & Documentation, Respect for Artists & Communities, and Accessibility & Education
